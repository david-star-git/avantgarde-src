Chlorine or Salt – What Belongs in Your Pool?

2026-02-05 1 min read
Pool Technology Home Construction Maintenance
Pool Chlorine Salt Electrolysis Water Quality Paraguay

It gets hot in Paraguay, and if you're building, you can't avoid the pool topic. Before you jump in the water, you need to make a decision: Classic chlorine or salt electrolysis?

Here are the facts without half-truths, so your pool project doesn't turn into a never-ending construction site.

1. Classic Chlorine: The Manual Method

Here, you're the chemist yourself. You dose chlorine manually as tablets or granules.

Fact is: It works immediately. But: You have to track the pH value like a shadow. If the pH value isn't right, the chlorine is ineffective, but still burns your eyes.

Honest opinion: Cheap to purchase, but time-intensive. Anyone who neglects the chemistry will have green water within 24 hours.

2. The Chlorine Generator (Salt Electrolysis)

A saltwater pool is not chlorine-free. The chlorine generator simply produces the chlorine on-site from salt that we add to the water.

The process: The electrolysis cell splits the salt (NaCl). Fresh chlorine is created.

The comfort: The salt concentration is minimal (approx. 0.4%). That's about as much as in a tear – you can barely taste it, but the skin feels extremely soft.

IMPORTANT TECHNICAL NOTE: Anyone who has a chlorine generator must never add regular chlorine on their own initiative! This can immediately destroy the generator's cell. The system is self-sufficient – anyone who mixes in foreign chemicals will pay for it in the end.

Why This Matters to Us

Whether home construction or pool technology: A system is only as good as its planning. Anyone who builds cheap components into a saltwater system risks corrosion damage. Anyone who chlorinates manually but has no time for maintenance loses water quality.

At Avantgarde, we build for longevity. A pool should bring relaxation, not additional work. That's why we rely on technology that's solid and reliable.
